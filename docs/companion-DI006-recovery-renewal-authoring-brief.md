## 0. Purpose — and its hard boundary

The **Recovery** and **Renewal** phases are canonical phases of the Relationship Life Cycle™ Framework (owner ruling, DI-006), but MKB v2.1 contains **zero competencies and zero behavioral indicators** for them. Every other layer of the Companion is complete and waiting on this gap. This brief scopes the framework authoring required to close it.

**What engineering / AI has NOT done, and must not do:**
- We have **not** authored any Recovery or Renewal competency, name, or definition.
- We have **not** authored any behavioral indicator for these phases.
- We do **not** decide the developmental *content* of these phases in any way.

Authoring competencies is **extending the framework** — reserved entirely for the qualified framework author, under theory review. This brief supplies only the **structural shell**: the pattern the existing four phases follow, ID conventions, blank templates, governance path, and the integration plan. The developmental substance is yours.

> Standing constraint from the framework governance: *do not modify or expand the framework except through the framework author under theory review; do not invent competencies; leave blank and report rather than fabricate.* This brief exists precisely so the blank is filled by the right hand.

## 2. The structural pattern to mirror

Each phase in the framework follows this layered shape:

```
Phase (fixed)
  └─ Developmental Task (fixed: Healing / Reengagement)
       └─ Competencies — across the 6 canonical Domains
            └─ Behavioral Indicators (~3 per competency)
                 └─ [downstream] Lessons · Practices · Reflections · Assessment Items · Recommendation Rules
```

**The 6 canonical domains are fixed and unchanged** — do not add or rename domains:
`Communication (COM) · Trust (TRU) · Emotional Intimacy (EMI) · Conflict Management (CON) · Role Functioning (ROL) · Physical Intimacy (PHY)`.

**Reference distribution (non-binding — shows shape, not a quota).** Competency counts taper across the life cycle and vary by domain; author to the developmental work, not a fixed number:

| Phase | COM | TRU | EMI | CON | ROL | PHY | Total |
|-------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:-----:|
| Exploration | 7 | 7 | 5 | 6 | 6 | 5 | 36 |
| Exclusivity | 7 | 5 | 7 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 27 |
| Expansion | 6 | 5 | 6 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 26 |
| Expiration | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 22 |

For scale, Recovery and Renewal would plausibly land in a similar range — but domain coverage and count are **your** framework decisions (some domains may carry more of the Healing/Reengagement work than others; a domain may legitimately carry few or none in a given phase).

## 3. Deliverables (in dependency order)

### Tier 1 — Framework core (author + theory review) — *the blocker*
1. **Competencies** for **Recovery (Healing)** and **Renewal (Reengagement)**, across the 6 domains: for each — competency name, definition, domain, phase, developmental-task alignment.
2. **Behavioral indicators** for each competency (~3), each an observable behavior.

### Tier 2 — Curriculum & assessment (follows once Tier 1 is approved)
Once competencies + indicators exist and are approved, these layers can be authored (by curriculum/assessment teams) or drafted against the new competencies: lessons, developmental practices, journal prompts, assessment items, recommendation rules. **Tier 2 cannot begin until Tier 1 is approved** — everything keys off the competency IDs.

## 5. Constraints the content must satisfy

1. **Derive from the Theory Manual** — competencies must express the developmental work of **Healing** (Recovery) and **Reengagement** (Renewal) as the framework defines them.
2. **Use the 6 canonical domains unchanged** — no new or renamed domains.
3. **One developmental task per phase** — already fixed (Healing / Reengagement); do not introduce alternates.
4. **Do not redefine or duplicate existing-phase competencies** — Recovery/Renewal competencies may *build on or reference* earlier-phase competencies, but each must be distinct and phase-appropriate.
5. **No new phases; no framework structural change** — this fills the competency layer of two *existing* phases only.
6. **Blank-not-invented discipline** — if a domain genuinely carries no distinct competency for a phase, leave it empty and note why; do not pad.
